// 01 — The 2019 Milestone

The Button From Orbit

On June 3, 2019, cosmonaut Oleg Kononenko pressed a symbolic button aboard the International Space Station, officially concluding Russia's analog television broadcast — a decade-long, $4.2 billion infrastructure overhaul spanning 85 regions and 5,040 transmitter sites.

// 04 — Late vs. Advanced

The Paradox of Russian Digital TV

Russia completed its digital transition in 2019 — years after Western Europe. Yet the standard it chose, DVB-T2, was more advanced than what most early adopters deployed. Late to finish, but built for the future.

Soviet State Broadcasting

A monolithic two-channel system. Central Television broadcast identical content across 11 time zones — the same newscast, aired at different clock times, but always the same editorial signal from Moscow.

Vremya (Time) — flagship evening newscast, watched by 150M+ citizens nightly
2 national channels, 0 commercial advertising, 100% state editorial control
SECAM color system — the French standard, chosen as a political signal away from Western PAL

NTV — Russia's First Private Channel (1993)

The Wild 90s — Oligarch TV

The Soviet broadcast system shattered overnight. By 1993, NTV — Russia's first private channel — was on air, backed by Vladimir Gusinsky's Media-Most. Political satire, investigative journalism, and raw commercial television flooded Russian screens for the first time.

NTV's Kukly (Puppets) satirized Yeltsin and Chubais — unprecedented on Russian screens
50+ regional broadcasters emerge; advertising market grows from $0 to $1B+ in 7 years
↗ NTV History

Pervyi Kanal — State TV Consolidation 2000s

2000s — Consolidation Under State

The Kremlin systematically reacquired control of major channels. NTV passed to Gazprom-Media in 2001. ORT became Pervyi Kanal. The chaotic freedom of the 90s gave way to managed pluralism — competition within a state-sanctioned framework.

Gusinsky and Berezovsky forced into exile; their channels revert to state-aligned ownership
Reality TV boom: Dom-2 launches in 2004, airs continuously for 17+ years

DVB-T2 Digital Switchover (2009–2019)

The Federal Target Program digitized the entire country in a decade. Two free-to-air multiplexes — each carrying 10 channels — were broadcast from 5,000+ transmitters, reaching 98% of Russia's population.

Multiplex 1: Pervyi Kanal, Rossiya 1, Rossiya-K, NTV + 6 others — all free, all HD
120M+ DVB-T2 receivers distributed; set-top boxes provided free to vulnerable households

Russia's Streaming Revolution — 2019 to Now

The Streaming Era (2019–Present)

The analog switch-off paradoxically accelerated Russia's streaming revolution. With viewers already updating hardware, OTT platforms surged. Kinopoisk HD, Okko, KION, and ivi emerged as dominant players, investing heavily in Russian-language originals.

Russian OTT market reached $1.5B+ by 2022, growing 40–60% annually
Post-2022 exit of Netflix accelerated domestic platform investment in originals
